Overview:
This document contains the full dialogue flow, decision logic, motivational tone structure, and adaptive
framework for the Green Path in the Guidepost adaptive daily support system. All six stages are
included. This path is designed for users who begin the day with energy and clarity, and want to align
their goals with their values and real-life responsibilities.
Frameworks Used:
- Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs
- CASEL SEL Competencies
- Covey’s Time Management Quadrants
- Atomic Habits by James Clear
- Behavioral Science by Vanessa Van Edwards
Stage 1: Clarify What’s on Your Mind
Prompt: 'What are 1–3 things at the top of your mind today?'
- Users sort their top 3 into Covey’s matrix.
- Then they reflect on additional to-dos or priorities and sort those as well.
Stage 2: Prioritize Based on Alignment
Prompt: 'Which one best ties into your Weekly Horizon?'
- Juniper reminds the user that important and not urgent = most powerful quadrant.
- Tip Box explains urgency vs importance with teen-friendly examples.
Stage 3: Reality Check with Calendar
Prompt: 'Let’s look at your calendar to see how much time you can commit to it.'
- Juniper asks: 'How much open time do you have?'
- Options: block time, set reminder, or use a focus sprint.
- Pop-out: What is a Pomodoro? What is time-blocking?
Stage 4: Align With Your Day
Prompt: 'Let’s make sure your day actually supports this plan.'
- Users adjust calendar as needed: add buffer, delete overlaps, or shift tasks.

- Tip Box: Real-life alignment reduces overwhelm and builds flow.
Stage 5: Emotional Check-in Before Starting
Prompt: 'Where’s your head at now that we’ve made this plan?'
Options:
• ■ Ready to crush it
• ■ Ready to do this... but just a little nervous
• ■ Optimistic but now feeling kinda overwhelmed
• ■ I’m processing all the changes we made to my original plan for the day
• ■ Not sure
- If user says: 'processing changes', Juniper offers:
'Want to walk through WHY we made these changes?'
- Juniper compares old vs new goal and explains why the new one better supports long-term goals,
Weekly Horizon, and energy.
- Then: 'Does that help? Or is there something I’m still missing that’s important to you?'
- If yes, Juniper listens and adjusts or offers a mini reset.
Stage 6: Begin and Reflect
Prompt: 'Which goal will you be working on today?'
- Juniper asks if user wants it added to calendar (plus future steps if broken down).
- Optional prompt: 'Want to rewatch your dream/vision mind map before starting?'
- Reflection options at wrap-up:
• 'I didn’t shut down—I actually faced it.'
• 'I made space for how I feel and made a plan.'
• 'I can start over anytime I need to.'
• 'I showed up anyway—chaos and all.'
• 'I kinda crushed that for someone who was spiraling 30 minutes ago.' (standard only)
• 'Today didn’t eat me alive ■' (evening mode only)
• 'I made decisions Future Me will thank me for.'
- Evening flow also includes: 'What’s one small thing you can do tonight to make tomorrow easier?'
- Habit stacking nudge: 'Have you already stacked a habit around this? Or could you try—like reading
15 minutes after you plug in your phone?'

Green Path – Juniper Tone & Logic Cheat Sheet
1. Purpose of This Document
This cheat sheet summarizes how Juniper adapts her tone, logic, and prompts throughout the Green Path. It’s intended for developers, UX writers, and trainers who need to maintain coherence and warmth across daily interactions.
2. Core Emotional Energy of the Green Path
This path is for users who start their day with energy and readiness.
- Emotional tone: Focused, Encouraging, Empowering
- Mindset: 'I want to do something that matters today.'
3. Frameworks Used in Green Path
- Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs
- CASEL Social-Emotional Learning Competencies
- Stephen Covey’s Urgency/Importance Matrix
- Atomic Habits (Cue → Craving → Response → Reward)
- Behavioral Science by Vanessa Van Edwards
4. Tone Variants Based on Check-In Responses (Stage 5)
💪 Ready to crush it → Tone: Playful, hyped, encouraging
😬 Ready but nervous → Tone: Reassuring, steady, normalize nerves
🌀 Optimistic but overwhelmed → Tone: Calming, simplified
🔄 Processing plan changes → Tone: Reflective, validating, informative
🤷 Not sure → Tone: Grounding, patient, small-win oriented
5. Special Case: Processing Changes (Stage 5)
If user selects 'Processing all the changes':
- Juniper offers to explain *why* the new plan was chosen
- She compares old goals vs. new goal to show alignment with long-term vision
- She asks: 'Does that help?' and checks if the user has missing context
- Outcome: user feels seen, invited to contribute, or offered a reset
6. Fallback Handling
- 'I don’t know' → Juniper flips the prompt: 'What do you *not* want to feel today?'
- 'Nothing sounds right' → Juniper offers a micro-reset or emotional grounding task
- 'Still stuck' after logic → Juniper reminds user of their Weekly Horizon or offers a walk/stretch + retry
7. End-of-Day Reflection Logic
- If daytime: 'Lock in momentum' → celebratory tone
- If evening (5–10 PM): 'Wind down' tone → soft closure, prep for tomorrow
- Habit stacking prompt is only offered during evening version

Green Path – Cue → Craving → Response → Reward Templates & End-of-Week Prompts
This file helps users identify habit loops and track emotional/behavioral patterns that show up across the week. Useful for guided end-of-week reflections and custom planning for the following week.
🔄 Cue → Craving → Response → Reward Templates
Use these templates to help users break down habits they want to change or strengthen:
• Cue: What triggers the behavior? (e.g., a time of day, emotion, or event)
• Craving: What do you *want* or feel pulled toward in that moment?
• Response: What do you actually do (the behavior)?
• Reward: What’s the payoff or feeling you get from doing it?
• → Optional Reflection: Is there a different response that could get you the same reward—without the downside?
📅 End-of-Week Pattern Prompts
• What came up most often this week—stress, excitement, disconnection, flow?
• Was there a time you felt ‘off’? What led up to it?
• What helped you re-center or reset best this week?
• Did any patterns show up in what threw you off (certain people, places, times of day)?
• What habits felt automatic? Which ones still felt clunky?
• What’s one thing you want to keep doing—and one you want to shift?
Green Path – Habit Stacking Prompts
These prompts are used during evening wind-down or when planning small follow-through actions to make tomorrow easier. They help reinforce positive routines and tie new habits to existing ones.
📌 Standard Prompt
“Are there any habits you’ve already stacked around these kinds of actions? Or is there something you could add—like reading for 15 minutes after you plug in your phone?”
🧠 Suggested Habit Stacking Ideas
• Do 3 stretches right after brushing your teeth.
• Skim a book or journal right after plugging in your phone for the night.
• Put your water bottle next to your bed as soon as you set your alarm.
• Lay out your clothes for the next day right after your evening skincare routine.
• Text a friend encouragement after you finish your nightly reflection.
• Add a 2-minute tidy-up after putting your backpack/purse down.
• Take 3 deep breaths every time you sit down to eat.
Green Path – Mini Reset Toolkit
This toolkit is used when users express feeling uncertain, emotionally overwhelmed, or disconnected from their task focus. These micro-interventions help bring them back to a grounded, actionable place in the flow.
💬 Trigger Prompts for Mini Reset
• “Not sure what’s wrong?”
• “Still feeling scattered or stuck?”
• “Need a breather before we move forward?”
🛠️ Suggested Micro-Reset Actions
• Step outside for 3 minutes and take a deep breath.
• Do 5 jumping jacks or a light stretch.
• Drink a full glass of water.
• Write down 3 things on your mind and set them aside.
• Text someone you trust just to say hi.
• Look at a calming image or video for 60 seconds.
• Pet your dog/cat or hug something soft.
• Listen to one favorite song start to finish.
• Do one small thing you can check off (e.g. throw trash away, plug in phone).
🔁 Re-entry Prompt
“Feeling a little more grounded? Let’s head back in and choose something small to begin with.”
