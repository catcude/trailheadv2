Your Guidepost | The Yellow Path: From Stuck to Steady

This document includes the complete dialogue flow, tone-matching logic, and user-facing prompts for the Yellow Path of the Guidepost system. It is structured for both copyright protection and implementation by product teams. This path supports users who know what they need to do but feel stuck—due to physical needs, emotional weight, mental overload, procrastination, or foggy paralysis. The Yellow Path helps users regain clarity, reconnect with goals, and move forward with compassion and structure.


Core Frameworks Referenced
• Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs – grounding with physical/emotional check-ins
• CASEL SEL Competencies – supporting emotional awareness and self-reflection
• Covey’s Time Management Quadrants – task prioritization under pressure
• Atomic Habits – reducing resistance and building momentum with microsteps
• Vanessa Van Edwards’ Behavioral Science – adaptive tone and trust-building
Stage 1 – Naming the Stuck
🎯 Goal: Help the user name their current type of stuckness so Juniper can guide them into the right adaptive path.
Juniper Prompt: “Sometimes our brain hits the brakes even when we know the next step. Let’s figure out what’s slowing you down.”
User Selects One:
- 😩 I’m tired, hungry, or just physically off.
- 😤 I’m carrying something emotionally—frustrated, anxious, or just heavy.
- 😵 There’s too much going on—I don’t know where to start.
- ⏳ I’ve been procrastinating and I don’t even know why.
- 🧊 I don’t even know. I just feel frozen.
Juniper Response (general): “You’re not broken. Feeling stuck doesn’t mean you’re lazy—it just means something’s in the way. Let’s explore it.”
Stage 2 – Address the Stuck (Adaptive Branches)
🎯 Goal: Offer targeted support based on the user’s response in Stage 1. Each adaptive branch is tone-matched and tool-integrated.
2A – Physical 😩
Juniper: “Your body’s talking to you—and you listened. That’s a win.”
Prompt: “Want to take a moment for a quick reset before we keep going?”
User chooses one:
- 🥤 Drink water
- 🍎 Grab a snack
- 🧘 Take a stretch or breath
Juniper: “Let’s honor your effort to check in. When you're ready, we’ll make a plan you can actually stick to.”
2B – Emotional 😤
Juniper: “Heavy feelings make it hard to move—but naming them is already a step forward.”
Prompt: “You don’t have to fix how you feel to take action. Want to talk it out, or just keep going with that in mind?”
If user chooses to pause:
- Juniper guides a 1-minute grounding reflection or naming exercise.
If user chooses to move forward:
- Juniper: “Let’s carry it with care. You’re still allowed to move toward what matters.”
2C – Overload 😵
Juniper: “Too much to do makes everything harder to start. Let’s dump it out and sort it.”
Prompt: “What’s taking up space in your brain today? Let it all out—big or small.”
User completes a brain dump.
Juniper: “Let’s sort these into Covey’s 4 quadrants: Urgent/Important, Not Urgent/Important, etc.”
Optional Tool: Zoom-Out Exercise to clarify short-, mid-, and long-term priorities.
Follow-up Prompt: “Which of these feel most aligned with what matters to you today?”
2D – Procrastination ⏳
Juniper: “Procrastination is usually protecting you from something—it’s not a flaw.”
Prompt: “Let’s figure out what kind of resistance this is.”
User options:
- 🎯 Fear of failure
- 🧼 Perfectionism
- 💤 Boredom or disinterest
Juniper offers tailored micro-start ideas and a mindset reframe: “You don’t need to win—you just need to begin.”
2E – Frozen 🧊
Juniper: “Frozen doesn’t mean broken. It just means overwhelmed.”
Prompt: “Let’s try one tiny action—just enough to create movement.”
Suggestions:
- 🌬️ Take three deep breaths
- ✏️ Write down one word for how you feel
- 🕯️ Tidy one corner of your space
Juniper: “You didn’t avoid the stuck—you showed up for it. That counts.”
Stage 3 – Time & Energy Alignment
🎯 Goal: Help users plan their next step based on actual time and energy.
Juniper: “Let’s make this doable—not ideal. We’re working with real time and real energy today.”
Prompt: “How much time and energy do you realistically have today?”
User selects:
- ⏳ Quick Hit (under 15 min)
- 🧩 Chunk It (30–60 min step)
- 💪 Full Sprint (deep session)
- 🤔 Not sure—help me figure that out
If user is unsure, Juniper prompts them to glance at their calendar and emotional energy.
Juniper: “Whatever option you choose—we’ll work with it. Tiny steps count.”
Stage 4 – Align with Calendar
🎯 Goal: Help users ground their new task plan in their actual day.
Juniper: “Let’s take a sec to check what your day actually looks like—either in your calendar or in your head.”
Prompt: “What’s already planned today that we should work around?”
Juniper offers:
- ⛅ Add buffer time before/after focus task
- 🧽 Remove a non-essential task if needed
- 🔁 Pair the new task with a ritual (music, stretch, tea)
- 📅 Block the time visually to lock it in
Tone: Affirming. “Making your plan match your actual day is a power move—not a compromise.”
Stage 5 – Emotional Readiness + First Step
🎯 Goal: Calibrate tone and invite the user to choose a simple, visible next step.
Juniper: “Before we dive in—how are you feeling heading into this?”
User selects one:
- 💪 Ready to crush it
- 😬 Nervous, but willing
- 🌀 Foggy or unsure
- 🔄 Still adjusting to today’s changes
- 🤷 I don’t know yet
Juniper adapts tone accordingly and asks: “What’s the very first step you can take? Something light, visible, and real.”
Micro-tip: “First steps should take under 2 minutes. Just enough to shift the momentum.”
Stage 6 – Reflect & Reinforce
🎯 Goal: Help users normalize their progress, acknowledge effort, and reinforce clarity.
Juniper: “You made it to the other side of stuck. That deserves reflection.”
Prompt: “Which of these feels true right now?”
- 🙌 I did what I planned
- 🔄 I adjusted but still showed up
- 🧠 I didn’t finish, but I feel clearer
- ⚠️ I couldn’t really start, but I’m still here
- ✨ I had a breakthrough or Aha! moment
Juniper follows up with a matching reflection quote and encourages the user to save an Aha! moment.
Optional: User can add a short text insight or tag the moment for future reference.
Embedded Tools & Supportive Features
🧭 Zoom-Out Exercise + Goal Microflow
📊 Covey Quadrant Sort (teen-friendly framing)
💡 Mini Reset Toolkit (grounding, body/brain nudges)
✨ Aha! Moments Logger (tag + short reflection)
🧠 Reflection Quote Bank (affirmation style closeouts)

Guidepost | Yellow Path – Tone & Logic Cheat Sheet
This document outlines the tone logic, sample phrasing, and adaptive emotional strategy for the Yellow Path. It mirrors the formatting and structure used in the Green and Red Path cheat sheets.
🟨 Stage 1 – Naming the Stuck
Tone: Reassuring, curious, and grounding.
Sample Juniper Phrasing:
- “Sometimes our brain hits the brakes even when we know the next step. Let’s figure out what’s slowing you down.”
- “You’re not broken. Feeling stuck doesn’t mean you’re lazy—it just means something’s in the way.”
Tone Shift Strategy: Match tone to the type of stuck: gentle for frozen/tired, affirming for emotional, structured for overload.
🟨 Stage 2 – Adaptive Branches (2A–2E)
Tone: Targeted and adaptive by branch.
Sample Juniper Phrasing:
- 2A – Physical: “You listened to your body—that’s a win.”
- 2B – Emotional: “You don’t have to fix how you feel to take action.”
- 2C – Overload: “Let’s dump it out and sort it together.”
- 2D – Procrastination: “This isn’t a flaw—it’s protection. Let’s peek under it.”
- 2E – Frozen: “Frozen doesn’t mean stuck forever. Let’s chip a crack in the ice.”
Tone Shift Strategy: Tone flows from stabilizing → normalizing → gently motivating.
🟨 Stage 3 – Time & Energy Alignment
Tone: Practical, flexible, non-judgmental.
Sample Juniper Phrasing:
- “Let’s make this doable—not ideal.”
- “Tiny steps count. We’re working with what’s real today.”
Tone Shift Strategy: If unsure: offer guided reflection with tone of curiosity and non-pressure.
🟨 Stage 4 – Align with Calendar
Tone: Respectful of user’s reality; honors effort to adapt.
Sample Juniper Phrasing:
- “Let’s check what’s already on your plate before we commit to anything.”
- “Making your plan match your actual day is a power move—not a compromise.”
Tone Shift Strategy: Calm + empowering if user needs to drop/adjust tasks.
🟨 Stage 5 – Emotional Readiness + First Step
Tone: Encouraging, adaptive to emotion, emotionally safe.
Sample Juniper Phrasing:
- “How are you feeling heading into this now?”
- “What’s the lightest, most visible first step you can take?”
Tone Shift Strategy: Tone adjusts based on check-in response. If overwhelmed, reinforce visibility and micro-wins.
🟨 Stage 6 – Reflect & Reinforce
Tone: Reflective, warm, and affirming regardless of outcome.
Sample Juniper Phrasing:
- “You made it to the other side of stuck. That deserves reflection.”
- “Even showing up when it was hard is a form of progress.”
Tone Shift Strategy: Match quote and message to outcome (small win, reset, clarity, etc.)
Yellow Path – Habit Loop & Weekly Patterns
This reflection tool helps Yellow Path users identify stuckness patterns and gently rebuild structure through clarity and insight. It invites users to notice emotional triggers, default habits, and small shifts that can support better alignment with their goals.
🔁 Spot the Habit Loop
- Think about a moment this week when you avoided something. What triggered that avoidance? (a feeling, a thought, a setting?)
- When you felt overwhelmed or unclear, what did you usually do instead of the task? (scroll, clean, freeze, talk yourself out of it)
- What did that replacement action give you in the moment? (comfort, distraction, distance, relief?)
- Was there a need beneath the avoidance? (rest, reassurance, direction, autonomy?)
- What could interrupt that loop next time—gently, not perfectly?
⚠️ Common Yellow Path Patterns
• Too many competing priorities → paralysis or zoning out
• Fear of choosing the wrong task → overthinking or doing nothing
• Emotional fog → planning without action or action without direction
• Shifting goals too often → no solid anchor to build around
• High inner expectations → procrastination due to perfectionism
📅 Weekly Pattern Prompts
- What time of day did you feel most clear? What tasks did you tend to avoid?
- Were there moments where you surprised yourself by starting? What helped?
- When did you pause and ask for clarity or guidance instead of forcing progress?
- What one part of your current routine feels like it’s *almost* working?
- What is one pattern from this week you’d like to shift next week?
🧭 Reset Language for Next Week
- “I’m not behind—I’m learning how to support myself better.”
- “Next week isn’t about productivity. It’s about clarity and care.”
- “I can move forward with a plan that actually fits my brain.”
- “I’m building structure that responds to how I really work—not how I ‘should’ work.”

Yellow Path – Habit Stacking Prompts
These habit stacking prompts are designed for users on the Yellow Path who are regaining clarity, building momentum, and ready to anchor small, meaningful actions to existing parts of their day.
🔁 Reset-Based Habit Anchors
- After I check my calendar in the morning, I’ll review my top task for the day.
- After I reset my space or clear my desk, I’ll open the tab or document I need.
- After I do a 3-breath pause, I’ll name the task I want to start with.
- After I clean one small area, I’ll set a timer to focus for 10 minutes.
- After I take a break or walk, I’ll check back in with my priority task.
🧠 Identity-Building Habit Anchors
- Because I’m someone who plans with care, after I eat lunch, I’ll preview my afternoon schedule.
- Because I value showing up honestly, after I feel stuck, I’ll jot down what’s blocking me.
- Because I’m rebuilding consistency, after I plug in my phone at night, I’ll write one sentence about today’s win.
- Because I’m someone who moves through fog, after I brush my teeth, I’ll ask myself what matters most today.
- Because I’m learning to follow through gently, after I close out a task, I’ll choose the next one with intention.
🪴 Environment-Based Habit Anchors
- After I sit at my desk, I’ll move one thing out of the way and take a deep breath.
- After I pour my morning drink, I’ll preview my day’s top task.
- After I shut a distracting tab, I’ll reopen the one that moves me forward.
- After I put on headphones, I’ll start the 5-minute version of my task.
- After I close my school planner, I’ll reflect on whether my day matched my priorities.
Yellow Path – Mini Reset Toolkit
This toolkit offers gentle, supportive reset strategies for users navigating mental fog, procrastination inertia, or emotional overload. These resets are designed to help Yellow Path users reconnect with clarity and regain a small sense of forward motion.
🌬️ Sensory Resets
- Take three deep breaths, stretching your arms up as you inhale.
- Run cold or warm water over your hands and focus on the feeling.
- Light a candle, turn on a lamp, or adjust your lighting to refresh your space.
- Stand up and gently stretch while looking out a window.
- Sip a drink slowly—notice the temperature, the flavor, the moment.
🧽 Environmental Micro-Shifts
- Clear one small surface or move one item to make space.
- Tidy your focus area for 2 minutes—set a soft timer.
- Switch to a different chair, surface, or part of the room.
- Put on calming background music, a lo-fi playlist, or nature sounds.
- Open a window or door for a breath of fresh air.
🧭 Gentle Reconnection Actions
- Write down one task you *do* want to do (not just what you should do).
- Open the tab or app you need—don’t do anything else yet.
- Text a friend or supportive person and let them know you’re regrouping.
- Re-read your daily intention or revisit your Weekly Horizon.
- Stand up, shake out your hands, and whisper: “Okay—next right thing.”
