Trailhead
Product Brief — Guidepost adaptive support system
Prepared July 2026 • Founder: Cat Cude • Source: Parker L Corp Blueprint Session, Guidepost design
documents
The One-Liner
Trailhead is an adaptive daily guide that helps students regulate their nervous systems, build
executive functioning skills, and keep up academically — all in one place, grounded in
peer-reviewed science, and priced so that the kids who need it most can actually have it.
Why It Exists
Cat is a teacher who struggled in school herself — not because she lacked ability, but because
she lacked support. As an educator, she watched the same pattern repeat: students carrying
trauma, instability, or unmet basic needs arrive at school with dysregulated nervous systems.
Their performance says almost nothing about what they're capable of. A hungry kid worried about
their siblings' safety cannot care about erosion or multiplication facts — and when school responds
with discipline instead of support, the cycle of suspension and labeling dictates their path.
Meanwhile, even high-achieving students are burning out. They've learned to perform, but no one
has taught them to manage their minds, emotions, and priorities. Parents are stretched thin and
feel they've lost their voice. Education is polarizing, and access increasingly follows money.
Trailhead is the interruption: an accessible, science-based guide that teaches students what they
were never taught — how to notice what they need, process what's hard, and move forward
anyway.
What It Is
The core product is Guidepost, an adaptive check-in and dialogue system led by Juniper, a
warm, real, non-clinical guide character. Each day, Juniper meets the user where they are and
routes them down one of four adaptive paths:
Green Path From Intention to Alignment — for users with energy and clarity who need
structure. Priority sorting (Covey quadrants), calendar alignment, habit
stacking, reflection.
Yellow Path From Stuck to Steady — for users who know what to do but can't start. Five
adaptive branches: physical, emotional, overload, procrastination, frozen.
Blue Path From Disconnection to Awareness — for the “I'm fine” user. Gentle surface
scans, mood-matching, quiet needspotting, soft re-entry.
Red Path From Overwhelm to Ownership — for emotionally flooded or shut-down
days. Regulation first (sleep, food, hydration), name the weight, plan-or-reset
branching, and resilience reflections (with escalation safeguards).
Every path is grounded in the same frameworks: Maslow's hierarchy of needs, CASEL SEL
competencies, Covey's time-management quadrants, Atomic Habits, and behavioral science. The
dialogue teaches nervous system regulation without calling it that — through prompts,
redirections, and tools, not lectures.
Onboarding begins with Discover Your Path — a 5-minute intropersonal quiz based on the Big
Five personality dimensions (openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness,
emotional resilience) that teaches Juniper how each person communicates and learns,
personalizes the experience, and later powers tutor matching. Answers are confidential and used
only to shape the experience.
Around the dialogue sits a dashboard of reusable tools — habit tracker, streak system, Weekly
Horizon planner, goal microflow tracker, Aha! moment logger — plus a community layer for
students, parents, and teachers, and eventually AI-assisted tutor matching.
Who It's For
• Primary — the student who has tried. Teens who genuinely want to do better and can't
figure out how. What they gain first is hope: feet back on the ground, able to breathe, not so
alone.
• The high achiever who is quietly unwell. Performers with structure and success on paper
who are burning out underneath.
• Parents — stretched thin, worried, unsure how to guide kids in today's world.
• Teachers & schools — who need an affordable, scalable way to give every student tailored
social-emotional support.
What Makes It Different
Trailhead sits at the intersection of academics + neuroscience + social-emotional learning.
Competitors pick one lane: therapy-minded apps skip executive functioning and academics;
academic apps ignore regulation and emotion. Trailhead treats the student as a whole picture,
because that's how struggling actually works.
“You're so real. You get it. I don't have to pretend.” — the words students use about
Cat's classroom are the product's north star.
Brand Voice (In Brief)
• Safe, not soft. Warm but direct. Real over polished.
• Non-hierarchical. Never lectures, never talks down, never clinical.
• Honest about difficulty. Growth is uncomfortable; the app is a guide, not a magic wand.
• Whole-person. A student is more than their grades.
• Anti-money-grab. Transparent pricing, easy cancellation, no dark patterns — ever.
Look & Feel
Less ed-tech, more adventure guide. The visual direction is Patagonia-catalog: clean white
backgrounds, generous breathing room, rounded cards, outdoor photography, mountain
silhouettes, and topographic/map elements. The confirmed palette: Sunset Coral (#F9A971) and
Bright Orange (#FB6526) as primary accents, Deep Sky Blue (#0165A5), Soft Lavender
(#A68DEB), Rich Indigo (#3B2089), Charcoal Gray (#4A4A4A), and Golden Sand (#D0B280).
Opening Trailhead should feel like opening a hiking journal, not a homework app.
Business Model
Freemium. A genuinely useful free tier (daily check-in with limits), an accessibly-priced paid tier
(unlimited conversations, full dashboard tools, community), and a future school/institutional tier so
districts can provide Trailhead to every student. It must be cheap, because the kids who need it
most don't have money.
Roadmap at a Glance
• Phase 1 (30 days): Landing page live, auth, core Guidepost check-in with adaptive paths,
mobile-first, download + referral incentive.
• Phase 2 (90 days): Community containers for students, parents, and teachers with overlap
spaces; chat history; dashboard tools.
• Phase 3 (180 days): Tutor matching by personality, needs, and interests — the original vision,
completed.
Success Looks Like
People using it. Feedback flowing. The beginnings of real community. And the moments that
anchor everything: a student who says the work helped them see themselves clearly — or, as one
student once wrote to Cat, that it saved their life.
