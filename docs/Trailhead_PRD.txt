Trailhead
Product Requirements Document — v1.0
July 2026 • Founder: Cat Cude • Guidepost adaptive support system, landing page, and community platform
This PRD consolidates the Parker L Corp Blueprint Session (Steady Build Q&A), the Trailhead Brand
Voice Report, the project CLAUDE.md, the four Guidepost path design documents (Green, Yellow, Blue,
Red), and the Guidepost Reusable Tools master list into a single build specification.
1. Overview & Vision
Trailhead is an adaptive daily support app for students (with parent and teacher layers) that combines
nervous system regulation, executive functioning, and academic support in one experience. The heart of
the product is Guidepost, a structured adaptive dialogue system fronted by Juniper, a warm, honest
guide character. Guidepost meets each user where they actually are — energized, stuck, numb, or
emotionally heavy — and routes them down a matched path built on peer-reviewed frameworks.
The founding insight: a student's school performance often has little to do with capability and everything
to do with regulation and unmet needs. A kid who is hungry or scared cannot learn multiplication facts.
Trailhead is designed as the affordable, judgment-free interruption to that cycle — a guide, a place to be
heard, and a mirror that helps students see themselves and their situations more clearly.
1.1 Product Pillars
• Adaptive, not generic. Dialogue branches by emotional state, energy, and need. Tone shifts with
the user.
• Science underneath, plain language on top. Maslow, CASEL SEL, Covey quadrants, Atomic
Habits, and behavioral science drive the logic; the user never gets a lecture. Regulation is taught
“without calling it that.”
• Whole-picture. Academics, emotion, and executive function are treated together, never separated.
• Equitable by design. Cheap enough for schools to give every student. No dark patterns, easy
cancellation.
• A guide, not a magic wand. The product is honest that growth is uncomfortable; it structures the
discomfort rather than promising to remove it.
1.2 Goals & Non-Goals
Goals
• Ship a mobile-first web app where a student can complete a daily adaptive check-in in under 5
minutes and leave with one clear, realistic next step.
• Make every visitor to the landing page “see their reflection” before signup via an adaptive, interactive
hero moment.
• Establish community containers for students, parents, and teachers with intentional overlap spaces.
• Build a safety layer that recognizes “deep water” and routes users to human help without shame.
Non-Goals (v1)
• Trailhead is not therapy and does not diagnose, treat, or replace counseling.
• No native iOS/Android apps in Phase 1 (PWA-quality responsive web instead).
• No open-ended, unbounded chatbot: Guidepost is a structured, path-based dialogue with
LLM-assisted adaptivity, not a blank chat window.
• Tutor matching is Phase 3, not v1.

3. Core Experience: The Guidepost Daily Check-In
The daily check-in is the product's spine. Juniper opens with a low-pressure prompt, reads the user's
state, and routes to one of four paths. Every path follows the same six-stage skeleton so users build a
familiar rhythm while the content adapts.
3.1 Universal Six-Stage Skeleton
• Stage 1 — Meet the state. Name what's present (top of mind, type of stuck, or surface scan).
• Stage 2 — Address it. Adaptive branch: sort, ground, reset, or gently probe based on Stage 1.
• Stage 3 — Time & energy reality check. Plan against real time and real energy, not ideals.
• Stage 4 — Calendar alignment. Buffer, remove, pair with ritual, or block time. “Matching your plan to
your actual day is a power move, not a compromise.”
• Stage 5 — Emotional readiness + first step. Check-in calibrates Juniper's tone; user picks a visible
first step (under 2 minutes).
• Stage 6 — Reflect & reinforce. Outcome-matched reflection quotes, Aha! moment capture, optional
habit stacking; evening variant winds down and preps tomorrow.

3.3 Juniper: Character & Tone System
Juniper is the product's voice — real, warm, non-clinical, never hierarchical. Tone is a first-class system
requirement: each stage of each path specifies a tone, and Stage 5 check-ins re-calibrate it.
User signal Juniper's tone
Ready to crush it Playful, hyped, encouraging
Ready but nervous Reassuring, steady; normalize nerves
Optimistic but overwhelmed Calming, simplified
Processing plan changes Reflective, validating, informative — explains the why, asks
what's missing
Not sure / foggy Grounding, patient, small-win oriented
Sample register (from path documents): “You're not broken. Feeling stuck doesn't mean you're lazy — it
just means something's in the way.” • “Procrastination is usually protecting you from something — it's not
a flaw.” • “Frozen doesn't mean broken. It just means overwhelmed.” Copy at this quality bar is a launch
requirement, not a nice-to-have.
3.4 Discover Your Path: Intropersonal Onboarding Quiz
A 5-minute quiz, offered at onboarding (“Explore the App First” is always available as a skip), that profiles
the user across the Big Five dimensions using teen-friendly scenario questions (A/B/C format):
openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, and emotional resilience.
• Purpose 1 — Communication calibration. Results teach Juniper how this user prefers to be talked
to (e.g., structure-seeking vs. flexible; needs reassurance vs. wants directness) and feed the tone
system alongside per-session check-ins.
• Purpose 2 — Personalized learning. Content and tool suggestions match learning and work style
(framed in-app as Personalized Learning, Skill Development, Community Connection).
• Purpose 3 — Tutor matching (Phase 3). The personality profile is a core input to
personality/needs/interest matching.
• Privacy: answers are confidential and used only to enhance the experience — this promise appears
in the UI and must hold in the data model (RLS; never exposed to parents, teachers, or tutors without
explicit consent).
• Results are adjustable: users can retake the quiz or override how Juniper talks to them in settings.
The profile informs tone; it never boxes the user in or appears as a label (“you're an introvert”) in
dialogue.
4. Reusable Tools
4.1 In-Dialogue Tools (surface inside path flows)
• Micro-Needs Menu — subtle support prompts for physical/emotional needs (stretch, silence, water,
reassurance, direction).
• Mood-Matching Visual — color wheel / metaphor deck / body map for naming a current state.
• Gentle Focus Anchor — sensory grounding or movement cues to refocus attention.
• Covey Quadrant Sorter — teen-friendly visual urgent/important matrix.
• Aha! Moment Tracker — tag insights and clarity moments; short text + tag for future reference.
• Evening Wind Down Prompts — end-of-day reflection, tomorrow preview, habit stack, intentional
pause.
• Start Small Planner — breaks big tasks into light, visible, under-2-minute first steps.
• Mini Reset Toolkit — sensory resets, environmental micro-shifts, gentle reconnection actions;
triggered on overwhelm or “still stuck.”
4.2 Dashboard Tools (standalone, habit-building)
• Habit Tracker — simple daily/weekly checklist of self-selected habits.
• Streak System — gamified consistency with a reset option and gentle, shame-free messaging.
• Goal Microflow Tracker — short-, mid-, and long-term goals with progress by category.
• Weekly Horizon Planner — week at a glance; allocate focus by intention; the anchor other flows
reference.
• Progress Reflection Tools — optional mood/progress/friction check-ins with course corrections,
including end-of-week pattern prompts and habit-loop (cue ® craving ® response ® reward)
templates.
5. Landing Page Requirements
One job: the visitor immediately sees their reflection. The hero must be adaptive/interactive — a small
taste of the Guidepost check-in itself (e.g., “What's at the top of your mind today?” with a live, gentle
response) rather than static marketing.
• Hero: interactive reflective moment mirroring Juniper's opening; no stock-photo hero.
• Problem section: name the reality (dysregulation, burnout, inequity) plainly, without preachiness.
• Solution section: the three-way intersection, explained the way Cat explains it to a stranger.
• How it works: a tangible sample exchange from a real path (e.g., Yellow Stage 1 ® 2D).
• Community seed: containers for students, parents, teachers — coming, honest about timing.
• Pricing: transparent freemium; explicit easy-cancel promise (direct answer to the anti-money-grab
value).
• CTA: low-pressure (“Try it. No tricks, no pressure.”); referral incentive surfaced post-signup, not as a
gate.
5.1 Design System — Patagonia-Inspired, Adventure Not Ed-Tech
The look should feel like opening a Patagonia adventure guide or hiking journal: clean white
backgrounds, generous breathing room, rounded cards, outdoor photography, mountain silhouettes, and
topographic/map elements. Coral is the primary accent. WCAG 2.1 AA contrast applies to every pairing.

6. Functional Requirements
6.1 Accounts & Roles
• Supabase Auth: email/password + Google OAuth. Roles: student, parent, teacher (in a profiles table,
not auth metadata).
• Onboarding: role selection (Student / Parent / Educator) on a welcome screen, display name, then
the optional Discover Your Path quiz (with an “Explore the App First” skip). Collect the minimum data
possible; assume minors and act accordingly (COPPA/FERPA-aware posture, parental consent flow
where required).
• Row Level Security on every table. A student's check-ins, reflections, and chats are visible only to
them — no parent/teacher surveillance of dialogue content.
6.2 Guidepost Engine
• Path router: entry check-in maps user state ® Green / Yellow / Blue / Red; supports mid-flow path
shifts per the Blue Stage 3 logic.
• Stage state machine: six stages per path with branch logic (e.g., Yellow 2A–2E), fallback handling,
and standard vs. evening (5–10 PM) variants.
• Hybrid content model: authored prompts, options, tip boxes, and reflection quote banks are canonical
content (versioned in the repo/DB); the LLM adapts phrasing, handles free-text user input, and
selects tone variants — it does not invent flow structure.
• LLM abstraction layer (lib/llm/provider.ts): provider-agnostic chat + streaming interface; provider set
by env var; no direct SDK imports outside the layer.
• Tool invocation: dialogue stages can summon in-dialogue tools (section 4.1) as structured UI, not as
text blobs.
• Aha! moments, reflections, and chosen habits persist to the dashboard.
6.3 Safety & Escalation (non-negotiable)
• “Depth of water” monitoring: detect crisis signals (self-harm, abuse, immediate danger) in free-text
input; pause the flow and surface human resources (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, Crisis Text Line,
trusted-adult prompts) in Juniper's steady voice.
• Clear boundary language: “I'm not a counselor, but here's someone who can help.” Never diagnose,
never prescribe, never attempt therapy for acute crises.
• Vulnerability safeguards: pathways cap reflective depth; Red/Blue flows always offer grounding exits.
• Privacy: no identifiable logging in safety telemetry; no behavioral ad tracking; no data sale, ever.
6.4 Community (Phase 2)
• Three containers (students, parents, teachers) plus shared overlap spaces.
• Posts and discussions with role-aware visibility; moderation tooling and report flows from day one of
the feature.
• Referral/invite incentive: reward for bringing someone in; framed as sharing help, not growth hacking.
6.5 Payments (Freemium)
• Free: daily check-in with a generous limit; core reflection; habit tracker.
• Paid (accessible price): unlimited Guidepost, full dashboard suite, community, history. Stripe; cancel
in two taps from settings; no retention dark patterns.
• School/institutional tier (future): bulk seats; grant-funding friendly.

8. Non-Functional Requirements
• Mobile-first: every flow excellent at 375px; check-in one-thumb operable.
• Performance: streamed LLM responses; interactive hero loads fast on school networks and old
devices.
• Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA; supports users with ADHD, anxiety, and learning differences (reduced
motion, clear focus states, no time pressure in dialogue).
• Reliability of tone: automated eval suite that regression-tests Juniper's outputs against the voice
guide before prompt changes ship.
• Stack: Next.js App Router + Tailwind, Supabase, Vercel; TypeScript strict; Server Components by
default.

10. Phased Roadmap
• Phase 1 — MVP (30 days): Landing page with interactive reflective hero; auth + role selection
welcome screen; Discover Your Path quiz (skippable); Green and Yellow paths end-to-end with Mini
Reset Toolkit, reflection quotes, and evening variants; Aha! tracker; habit tracker; safety layer v1;
freemium billing; Patagonia design system foundations (palette, type, cards).
• Phase 2 — Community & depth (90 days): Blue and Red paths (Red spec is complete; ship only
after dedicated safety review); community containers + overlap; Weekly Horizon planner; streaks;
goal microflows; referral incentive; progress reflection tools; dashboard journey map + progress rings.
• Phase 3 — Tutoring (180 days): Tutor profiles; personality/needs/interest matching powered by the
Big Five profile; scheduling; the completed original vision — academics + support + human
connection.
